# Inbox

The Inbox is your temporary thinking space.

Think of it as Dumbledore's Pensieve:
A place where thoughts can be stored before they disappear.

## Purpose

The only job of the Inbox is:

**Capture first. Decide later.**

Ideas, tasks, links, questions, problems, reminders, random thoughts...

Everything is allowed here.

## Rules

- Do not organize while capturing.
- Do not create categories.
- Do not search for the perfect location.
- Do not polish notes.

A messy note in the Inbox is better than a lost thought in your head.

## Examples

Good Inbox entries:

- "Maybe automate my backup process"
- "Look into Docker volumes"
- "Need to renew passport in 2027"
- "Idea: write article about my homelab setup"
- "Why does systemd do this?"

Bad Inbox behavior:

- Spending 10 minutes deciding whether something belongs in Zettelkasten or Projects.
- Creating folders for future possibilities.
- Rewriting notes before knowing if they matter.

## Processing

The Inbox is not permanent storage.

When you review it, every item gets one decision:

- Move it to an active project.
- Turn it into a routine.
- Store it as knowledge in the Zettelkasten.
- Turn it into output.
- Archive it.
- Delete it.

If no action is needed, let it go.

## Remember

The Inbox exists to protect your attention.

Capture quickly.
Organize later.
Keep working.
